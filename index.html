Who We Are

Born Bad,
Served Spectacular

The Wicked Wenches Cabaret is a collective of over twenty performers — actors, dancers, aerialists, acrobats, and all-around theatrical renegades — united by one conviction: live performance should shake you, delight you, and leave you absolutely breathless.

From showstopping group numbers set to Chicago, Moulin Rouge, and Beyoncé, to sizzling solo and duet acts where performers play, peel, and shimmy their way into your memory, WWC is adult entertainment in the truest, most magnificent sense. We add magicians, pole artists, belly dancers, aerialists, and the occasional snake charmer — because why wouldn't we?

We are proudly based at Minnsky Vaudeville Theatre in the Northeast Minneapolis Arts District, the twin cities' heartbeat for vaudeville, burlesque, and cirque arts.

Where We Perform

Our Home Stage

Minnsky Vaudeville Theatre
1517 Central Ave NE · Minneapolis, MN

The Wicked Wenches Cabaret is a resident company of Minnsky Vaudeville Theatre — the Northeast Minneapolis Arts District's premier home for burlesque, vaudeville, and cirque performance. The name is a knowing nod to both Minnesota roots and the legendary Minsky Brothers, vaudeville's original royalty.

Minnsky opened in the space formerly occupied by nimbus theatre on Central Avenue NE. It exists to reintroduce vaudeville and burlesque to the Twin Cities as living, vital theatrical traditions — not museum pieces, but evening-defining spectacles for adventurous adults.

ExperTease Fitness
Official Training Facility · Minneapolis

The rehearsal home of the Wicked Wenches and the wider Minnsky family of companies, ExperTease is the Twin Cities' premier alternative gym and performance training studio. Pole dance, aerial arts, belly dance, chair dance, and burlesque technique — all in one space.

Every eight weeks, ExperTease students perform at Minnsky in showcases that feed directly into the professional burlesque community. It's a pipeline that keeps the scene alive, fresh, and constantly surprising.

The City Behind the Curtain

The Minneapolis
Burlesque Scene

Minneapolis doesn't just have a burlesque scene. It has a burlesque community — over 500 performers strong, spanning actors, dancers, aerialists, acrobats, magicians, and performance artists who regularly cross into each other's art forms.

The Twin Cities burlesque revival traces much of its modern momentum to Minnsky Vaudeville Theatre and its constellation of resident companies. Jac Fatale — known on stage as Alina Rae — built Minnsky as a deliberate home for vaudeville, cirque, and burlesque in the Northeast Minneapolis Arts District, a neighborhood already thick with galleries, theaters, and working studios.

The Wicked Wenches Cabaret, the Minnsky Follies, and Cirque De Lune anchor the programming, but the broader scene reaches well beyond Central Avenue NE. Black Hearts Burlesque brings a darker, edgier aesthetic to venues across the metro. The Minneapolis Burlesque Festival, produced annually by Elektra Cute, draws performers from across the globe to showcase alongside hometown talent — a weekend of shows, workshops, and community that has become one of the year's most anticipated events.

Ground Zero Nightclub, the Ritz Theater, Patrick's Cabaret, the 331 Club, and the Luminary Arts Center have all hosted burlesque programming that serves the scene's different audiences — from the rowdy and irreverent to the theatrical and polished. Camp Bar in St. Paul has built its own reputation as a cabaret and community gathering space across the river.

ExperTease Fitness keeps the pipeline full: every eight weeks, a new class of students performs their first showcase, many of them stepping into a professional scene they quickly become part of. The result is a burlesque community that's genuinely multigenerational, multi-disciplinary, and utterly alive.
